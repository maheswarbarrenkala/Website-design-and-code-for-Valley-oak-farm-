History
This property was purchased in 2014 in a very neglected form. The north 5 acres were basically a motorcycle cross course, the ground was compacted and the only thing that grew were the very hardy weeds! In this side there is also a barn which was originally built in the 1890s which was in a very bad shape.
“From Neglected Land to New Beginnings”
The barn in this state was a hazard and we were afraid that our curious 9-year-old boy would hurt himself. So the first order of business was to repair the barn. After going through the permitting process – during which we were forced to have to meet the most recent Uniform Building Code for a 120 year old barn – we were patient, endured and the “new barn” was born.
Traded SF's rush for fields bathed in gold.  Our hands, once typing, now tend the earth we hold.  Fresh air, not sirens, fills our daily song. Simpler days, where the land whispers strong.  From concrete jungle to a life we now belong.
Soil tests revealed deficiencies. We embraced natural solutions!  Free wood chips from arborists became a year-long compost project, perfect for our new sheep.  The composted chips, along with minerals, helped break up the clay and jumpstart a healthy soil microbiome.
Over the years, our farm family grew! We welcomed a tiny Jersey calf, easily picked up in our arms. Simsim, as we named her for her beautiful tan coat, quickly blossomed into our gentle milk cow, a vital part of our life on the land.
